The PKA Philosophy:

The cornerstone of PKA’s business philosophy is quality, in terms of service and engineering design and documentation. Our commitment to excellence is demonstrated by PKA’s development of structural designs through thorough and careful technical analyses; the establishment of design schedules and management of manpower to achieve them; the production of drawings and specifications through teamwork and coordination with the other disciplines involved; the production of complete, coordinated and properly detailed construction drawings and specifications; close communication with the client and, during construction, the contractor; and the prompt response to questions and requests for information.

PKA is committed to teamwork on every project. Our staff works with the owner, architect, civil, mechanical and electrical engineers, and the contractor or construction manager to develop overall project solutions that meet the owner’s needs, goals and desires.

PKA is particularly attentive to the construction phase of projects. PKA strives to provide prompt, responsive service in connection with requests for information, questions and field issues to help maintain construction schedules and progress. One of our goals on every project is for construction to proceed as smoothly as possible.

The results of our approach can be measured in the timely production of design documents, attainment of accurate construction bids and project budgets, and the smooth execution of construction. The results are also reflected in the repeat business and referrals that PKA enjoys. That the majority of our business comes from existing clients and their referrals is a source of great pride to us.

As a principal, Mr. Wu has primary responsibility for the firm’s structural engineering projects related to civil structures. These projects include vehicular, pedestrian and golf cart bridges, pump stations, lift stations, retaining walls, environmental structuers, public works facilities and other civil structures.

Since joining the firm in 1997, Mr. Wu has been responsible for the structural design of bridges, environmental structures, water and wastewater facilities, retaining walls and a wide variety of building types. He has developed a strong reputation for responsive service and technical excellence, and has established himself as an outstanding engineer in the area of civil structures, particularly bridges and retaining structures.

Notable Projects Include:

  • Rollingwood Towne Center Bridge in Austin. Project consists of a single span, 80’ prestressed concrete I-beam bridge designed to support an 8’ wide x 30” deep landscaping planter in the median. Stone veneer arched facades were designed at both faces of the bridge to conceal the prestressed concrete beams.
  • McNeil-CMTA Overpass in Williamson County, Texas. The structure consists of a 3-span, 323’ bridge on a 60 degree skew over an existing Southern Pacific railway.
  • Discovery Boulevard Bridge in Cedar Park, Texas. Project consists of a 4-span, 134’ long precast concrete arch bridge with TxDOT decorative C411 railings and mechanically stabilized retaining walls. The bridge was designed to be a primary feature of the new Cedar Park downtown center.
  • Cimmarron Hills Trail West Bridge in Georgetown, Texas. Project consists of a 5-span, 400’ prestressed concrete I-beam bridge on a horizontal curve. The bridge was located along a golf course and included custom designed decorative railings covered with masonry stone veneer.
  • LCRA Chemical Storage Building in Bee Cave, Texas.
  • Winfield MUD No. 1-4 Wastewater Treatment Plant in Hays County, Texas
  • Sandy Creek Yacht Club Tram and Retaining Wall at Volente, Texas.
